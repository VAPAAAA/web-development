Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Northern Lights tours

The best time to see the Northern Lights is during the winter months (September to March) when the nights are longest. Peak visibility typically occurs between 10 PM and 2 AM under clear, dark skies.

The best locations are within the "aurora zone" - between 60 and 75 degrees north latitude. Iceland, northern Norway, and Finnish Lapland are prime viewing locations, offering clear skies and minimal light pollution.

Our tours typically range from 3-4 hours for evening viewings to 5-7 days for complete Northern Lights experiences. The duration can be customized based on your preferences and travel plans.

We recommend warm, layered clothing, winter boots, gloves, and a hat. For photography, bring a camera with manual settings and a tripod. We provide additional thermal gear when necessary.
